> **Every section below must be answered before this PRD is "done."** Where the brief didn't say and the answer changes what gets built, the skill should have asked the human — not invented it. Anything still unknown goes in **Open questions**, never faked.

## 1. Problem & why now
<The pain, who has it, and why it's worth solving now.>

## 2. Target users / personas
<Concrete users. For each: who they are, the situation they're in, what they need. One or two real personas beats a long list of categories.>

## 3. The one differentiator
<The single thing this product must do better than the alternatives — its reason to exist. This typically becomes non-negotiable invariant #1 in AGENTS.md. If it's fuzzy, the whole PRD is at risk; sharpen it here.>

## 4. Goals & success metrics
<How you'll know it worked. Measurable where possible (adoption, time saved, error/latency budget); a clear qualitative bar where not. Include the anti-goal: what you are NOT optimizing for.>

## 5. Scope
### In scope (MVP)
<The smallest set of capabilities that delivers the differentiator to a real user.>
### Out of scope (for now)
<Explicit non-goals — things deliberately deferred, to keep the MVP boundary honest.>

## 6. Key features / capabilities
<The handful that deliver the differentiator, roughly prioritized. For each: one line on what it does and the user need it serves. Each of these will later become one or more specs in docs/specs/.>

| Priority | Capability | What it does / need served |
|---|---|---|
| P0 | <name> | <one line> |

## 7. Constraints
<Platform (web/mobile/desktop/CLI), required tech stack or vendors, timeline, budget, team size/skills, compliance/privacy/security requirements. These bound every downstream design decision.>

## 8. Assumptions & risks
<What you're betting on (assumptions) and what could sink it (risks), with a rough sense of likelihood/impact and any mitigation. State assumptions explicitly so they're correctable.>

## 9. Open questions
<Anything still unresolved. Carry these forward — a visible open question is better than an invented answer. Resolve them into decisions in docs/DECISIONS.md as they're answered.>

<The very first feature to build after this PRD is approved — the one that proves the differentiator soonest. This becomes the first spec in docs/specs/ and the first entry in docs/state/CURRENT.md.>
